# ShopWorld Business and GTM Research Questions

## 1. Purpose

This document lists the business, market, customer, pricing, positioning, and go-to-market research questions needed before turning ShopWorld from a technical RLE concept into a commercial product.

The central GTM question is:

> Who urgently needs proof that Shopify agents are safe and capable, and what evidence will make them pay?

## 2. Business Hypotheses to Validate

### 2.1 Core Market Hypothesis

Shopify merchants are interested in AI agents, but adoption is blocked by trust, safety, and uncertainty about write-access risk.

Questions:

1. How many merchants are actively evaluating AI agents today?
2. What store revenue range has enough operational pain and enough risk sensitivity to pay for evaluation?
3. Which merchant segments are most AI-curious but not yet trusting?
4. Is agent-readiness evaluation a top-priority problem or a nice-to-have?
5. What current events trigger interest: support backlog, fulfillment mistakes, growth plateau, hiring constraints, agency recommendations, or AI vendor outreach?

### 2.2 Primary Buyer Hypothesis

The primary buyer is likely a Shopify merchant/operator, but agencies, agent startups, and Shopify app developers may be faster early adopters.

Questions:

1. Who owns the budget for AI agent evaluation inside a Shopify merchant business?
2. Is the buyer the founder, head of ecommerce, ops lead, CX lead, or technical lead?
3. Do merchants already have budget for QA/testing/sandboxing automation?
4. Would agencies pay to evaluate agents before recommending them to clients?
5. Would agent startups pay for training/eval infrastructure before merchants pay for readiness reports?
6. Would model providers or commerce-agent vendors sponsor benchmark participation?

### 2.3 Trust Gap Hypothesis

Merchants need concrete evidence, not generic benchmark scores.

Questions:

1. What failures do merchants fear most?
2. Which evidence would change a merchant’s willingness to grant read/write scopes?
3. Are traces and failure replays persuasive to non-technical merchants?
4. Do merchants trust synthetic store simulations?
5. What makes a simulated benchmark credible: Shopify API fidelity, store-specific data import, third-party validation, public leaderboard, or agency endorsement?
6. Does a readiness score reduce fear, or do merchants need workflow-specific approvals?

## 3. Customer Discovery Questions

### 3.1 Merchant Operations

1. What are the top 5 recurring operational headaches in your store?
2. Which workflows consume the most human time each week?
3. Which workflows are most error-prone?
4. Which workflows have the highest financial downside when done wrong?
5. Which tasks are currently outsourced to agencies, VAs, 3PLs, or apps?
6. Which tasks are still founder-operated because trust is low?
7. What systems besides Shopify are part of daily operations?
8. How often do customer support issues require Shopify Admin actions?
9. How often do fulfillment or supplier issues show up first as customer complaints?
10. What operational edge cases do generic automations fail to handle?

### 3.2 Agent Interest and Current Usage

1. Are you currently using AI in store operations?
2. Which tools are you using: chatbots, helpdesk AI, Shopify apps, internal GPT workflows, automation platforms, or custom agents?
3. Are any AI tools allowed to write to Shopify today?
4. If not, why not?
5. What would you let an agent do read-only?
6. What would you let an agent draft for approval?
7. What would you let an agent automate without review?
8. Which permission scopes feel most dangerous?
9. Which permission scopes feel acceptable?
10. Have you had any AI or automation failures already?

### 3.3 Risk and Safety

1. What is the worst plausible mistake an AI agent could make in your store?
2. What mistake would make you uninstall an agent immediately?
3. What mistakes are tolerable if caught quickly?
4. How much monetary loss is acceptable in a test or limited rollout?
5. How important is customer trust compared with direct financial loss?
6. How do you currently review staff, VA, or agency actions?
7. Would you require approval for refunds, discounts, price changes, inventory edits, or customer emails?
8. What escalation rules should an agent follow?
9. What policies are written down today?
10. What policies are implicit founder judgment?

### 3.4 Readiness Report Value

1. Would you pay for an agent-readiness report before adopting an AI agent?
2. What would the report need to contain?
3. Would you prefer a simple pass/fail, scorecard, or detailed trace analysis?
4. Should scores be organized by workflow, permission scope, financial risk, or operational domain?
5. Would you share a readiness report with an agency, investor, board, or insurer?
6. Would a report influence purchasing decisions between agent vendors?
7. Would you rerun reports after prompt, policy, or app changes?
8. How often would ongoing evaluation be useful: one-time, monthly, before major campaigns, or after agent updates?
9. Would you want benchmarking against similar stores?
10. Would you want a recommended staged deployment plan?

## 4. Segment Research

### 4.1 Merchant Revenue Segments

Questions:

1. Below what revenue level is the problem not painful enough?
2. At what revenue level does operational complexity justify paid evaluation?
3. At what revenue level do merchants prefer custom consulting over self-serve tooling?
4. Which segment has the fastest buying cycle?
5. Which segment is most likely to share store data for private simulation?

Candidate segments to test:

- <$500k GMV: likely budget-constrained, high founder involvement.
- $500k-$2M GMV: may have pain but limited budget.
- $2M-$10M GMV: likely strongest initial merchant fit.
- $10M-$50M GMV: stronger budgets, more systems, slower sales.
- $50M+ GMV: enterprise concerns, custom procurement, compliance needs.

### 4.2 Vertical Segments

Questions:

1. Which vertical has the richest operational-agent use cases?
2. Which vertical has high return/refund complexity?
3. Which vertical has meaningful inventory and supplier complexity?
4. Which vertical avoids regulatory risk for MVP demos?
5. Which vertical has merchants willing to publicly discuss AI adoption?

Candidate verticals:

- Apparel and accessories.
- Beauty and personal care.
- Home goods.
- Pet supplies.
- Electronics accessories.
- Supplements and wellness, likely later due to regulated-claim risk.
- Food and perishables, likely later due to logistics complexity.

### 4.3 Buyer Personas

Questions:

1. Founder/operator: do they care most about trust, cost savings, growth, or control?
2. Head of ecommerce: do they care about workflow reliability and performance uplift?
3. CX lead: do they care about support quality and escalation reduction?
4. Ops lead: do they care about fulfillment, inventory, and supplier coordination?
5. Agency: do they care about client risk, vendor selection, and recurring service revenue?
6. Agent startup: do they care about eval credibility, training data, and faster product iteration?

## 5. Competitive and Alternative Research

### 5.1 Direct Alternatives

Questions:

1. Are there existing Shopify agent evaluation platforms?
2. Do AI support vendors provide sandbox evaluation?
3. Do Shopify agencies run manual agent QA?
4. Do agent startups build internal simulators?
5. Does Shopify itself offer sufficient dev-store/sandbox tooling?
6. Are there open-source retail benchmarks that customers already trust?

### 5.2 Indirect Alternatives

Questions:

1. Are merchants solving this by keeping agents draft-only?
2. Are merchants using human approval queues instead of evaluation?
3. Are agencies manually testing workflows on staging stores?
4. Are vendors relying on demos instead of rigorous tests?
5. Are merchants delaying adoption entirely?

### 5.3 Differentiation

Questions:

1. Is the strongest wedge the Shopify API simulator, the merchant-specific synthetic twin, or the readiness report?
2. Is the strongest technical moat the world model, task library, counterfactual generator, or store-import pipeline?
3. Would a benchmark without merchant-specific import be compelling?
4. Would merchant-specific private testing matter more than public leaderboard ranking?
5. Should the product be positioned as safety, QA, training, or ROI optimization?

## 6. Pricing and Packaging Research

### 6.1 Willingness to Pay

Questions:

1. Would merchants pay for a one-time readiness assessment?
2. Would merchants pay for ongoing monitoring/evaluation?
3. Would agencies pay per client/store?
4. Would agent vendors pay per eval run, per seat, or annual license?
5. Would model vendors pay for benchmark access?
6. Is value tied to avoided mistakes, labor savings, conversion uplift, or agent vendor selection?

### 6.2 Pricing Models to Test

Options:

- One-time readiness report.
- Monthly SaaS for ongoing agent evaluation.
- Per-store assessment fee.
- Agency package for multiple client stores.
- Vendor benchmark license.
- Usage-based scenario/eval runs.
- Enterprise custom simulator.
- Future profit/revenue-share deployment model.

Questions:

1. Which pricing model matches buying behavior?
2. Is a one-time report too transactional?
3. Does ongoing evaluation become necessary as agents, prompts, policies, and stores change?
4. What price feels credible but not risky for a merchant?
5. Would a free basic benchmark help acquire agent vendors?
6. Should merchant-specific import be paid only?

### 6.3 Packaging

Potential packages:

- Starter: generic benchmark, no store import.
- Merchant Readiness: private synthetic twin from read-only data export.
- Agency Pro: multi-store dashboards and client reports.
- Vendor Eval: benchmark suite, traces, and regression testing.
- Training/Gym: environment access for agent optimization.

Questions:

1. Which package is easiest to sell first?
2. Which package creates the best learning loop for product development?
3. Which package minimizes support burden?
4. Which package produces the strongest case studies?

## 7. GTM Wedge Research

### 7.1 Initial Beachhead

Questions:

1. Should the first wedge be merchants, agencies, or agent startups?
2. Which wedge has urgent pain and short sales cycle?
3. Which wedge provides the best feedback on world realism?
4. Which wedge creates credibility with merchants?
5. Which wedge is easiest to reach with founder-led sales?

Hypotheses:

- Agencies may be the best distribution wedge because they advise multiple merchants and already manage risk.
- Agent startups may be the fastest technical early adopters because they need eval/training loops immediately.
- Merchants may be the highest-value end buyer but need more trust and clearer ROI.

### 7.2 Positioning Tests

Messaging options:

1. “Crash-test your Shopify AI agent before it touches your store.”
2. “Know which AI workflows are safe to automate.”
3. “A private simulator for your Shopify store.”
4. “Agent readiness reports for Shopify merchants.”
5. “The benchmark for commerce agents.”
6. “Train and evaluate AI store managers in a simulated Shopify world.”

Questions:

1. Which message resonates most with merchants?
2. Which message resonates most with agencies?
3. Which message resonates most with agent vendors?
4. Does “reinforcement learning environment” help or confuse commercial buyers?
5. Should merchant-facing language avoid benchmark/RL terminology?
6. Should technical buyers see benchmark/RLE details separately?

### 7.3 Channels

Questions:

1. Can Shopify agencies introduce pilot merchants?
2. Are Shopify app developers a reachable partner channel?
3. Are AI-commerce communities active enough for launch?
4. Would a public benchmark attract model/vendor attention?
5. Would case studies with anonymized failure traces drive demand?
6. Could Shopify Editions/news cycles create launch timing?
7. Would investor/operator communities generate early adopters?

Potential channels:

- Founder-led outbound to Shopify Plus agencies.
- Direct outreach to AI-commerce agent startups.
- Shopify merchant communities.
- Ecommerce operator newsletters.
- LinkedIn content with failure trace examples.
- Public benchmark launch for vendor credibility.
- Partnerships with helpdesk/automation consultants.

## 8. Pilot Design Questions

### 8.1 Merchant Pilot

Questions:

1. What minimum data is required for a useful merchant-specific twin?
2. Will merchants grant read-only export access for products/orders/customers/support history?
3. What security review is required?
4. How long can onboarding take before interest drops?
5. What workflows should the first pilot test?
6. What output would make the pilot successful?
7. Would merchants pay for the pilot or expect it free?
8. Can results be anonymized into a case study?

Suggested pilot scope:

- Read-only import.
- 3 workflows.
- 20-30 scenarios.
- 2-3 candidate agents/prompts.
- Readiness report by workflow and permission.

### 8.2 Agency Pilot

Questions:

1. Would an agency use ShopWorld as part of agent/vendor selection?
2. Would an agency white-label reports for clients?
3. Would agencies want custom scenario authoring?
4. What recurring service could agencies build around this?
5. How many client stores would justify agency pricing?

### 8.3 Vendor Pilot

Questions:

1. Would agent vendors integrate ShopWorld into CI/regression testing?
2. What environment API would they need?
3. Would they pay for private scenario generation?
4. Would they publish benchmark scores?
5. What failure diagnostics would improve their product fastest?

## 9. Data, Privacy, and Trust Research

Questions:

1. What store data are merchants willing to export?
2. Is order history considered too sensitive?
3. Is customer data export a blocker even if anonymized?
4. Should the product avoid importing PII entirely?
5. What compliance posture is required for early customers?
6. Should evaluation run locally, in a private cloud, or hosted?
7. What data retention policy is acceptable?
8. Would merchants require SOC 2 before using store-specific import?
9. Can a synthetic-only version deliver enough value before import?
10. How should results explain that the simulator is not touching live store state?

## 10. Product-Led Growth and Benchmark Strategy

Questions:

1. Should there be a public generic ShopWorld benchmark?
2. Would a leaderboard attract agent vendors or distract from merchant sales?
3. Should benchmark tasks be public, private, or split?
4. How can public benchmark credibility translate into merchant trust?
5. Should merchants compare vendors on their private store twin rather than a public score?
6. Could a free browser demo show common agent failures convincingly?
7. Would publishing “agent failure modes in Shopify operations” generate inbound interest?

## 11. Sales Objection Research

Likely objections:

- “We are not ready for agents yet.”
- “We already use a helpdesk AI.”
- “Synthetic simulation will not match my store.”
- “I do not want to share store data.”
- “We can just test in a dev store.”
- “This sounds too technical.”
- “The agent vendor should provide this.”
- “I care about ROI, not benchmark scores.”

Questions:

1. Which objections appear most often?
2. Which objections are fatal vs addressable?
3. What proof resolves each objection?
4. Does an agency endorsement reduce objections?
5. Does a free generic eval reduce objections?
6. Does read-only import reduce data concerns enough?

## 12. ROI Research

Questions:

1. What is the economic value of safely automating support workflows?
2. What is the economic value of avoiding one bad refund/chargeback/oversell incident?
3. What labor cost can be reduced or reallocated?
4. What revenue uplift could come from faster support, better retention, fewer stockouts, or better promotions?
5. How should expected uplift and worst-case drawdown be estimated credibly?
6. Do merchants think in GMV, gross margin, contribution margin, cash, or time saved?
7. How much does a support backlog cost in churn/reviews/chargebacks?
8. What level of performance improvement justifies purchase?

## 13. Partnership Research

Questions:

1. Which Shopify agencies are already advising on AI adoption?
2. Which helpdesk vendors or consultants could partner?
3. Which agent startups need external evaluation credibility?
4. Which Shopify app ecosystem partners own relevant workflows?
5. Are there insurer/risk-management angles for autonomous commerce operations?
6. Could Shopify itself be a future partner or acquisition path?
7. Could benchmark participation become a badge for commerce agents?

## 14. Research Interview Guide

### Merchant Interview

1. Tell me about your store and team.
2. What operational work still depends on founder judgment?
3. Where are you already using AI or automation?
4. What would make you nervous about giving an agent Shopify access?
5. Which workflows would you most want automated?
6. Which workflows would you never automate?
7. How would you test whether an agent is safe?
8. If you saw a readiness report, what would need to be in it?
9. What store data would you share for a private simulation?
10. Would you pay for this? Under what pricing model?

### Agency Interview

1. Are clients asking about AI agents?
2. How do you currently evaluate AI tools before recommending them?
3. What agent failures would damage your client relationship?
4. Would a simulator/readiness report help your sales or delivery process?
5. Would you want to author client-specific scenarios?
6. Would you white-label this?
7. How would you price a service around it?

### Agent Vendor Interview

1. How do you test Shopify workflows today?
2. Do you have internal simulation or regression tests?
3. Which Shopify workflows are hardest for agents?
4. Would a deterministic RLE improve your development cycle?
5. What API/interface would you need?
6. Would you publish scores or use results privately?
7. What diagnostics are most valuable after a failed run?
